Moral System Stress Test

Test whether a Christian moral claim supplies the necessary and sufficient elements of an actual moral system, or whether the claim thins out into emotion, obedience, social strategy, or practical advice.

Diagnostic, not a final verdict. The audit asks what a moral system would need in order to be unequivocally distinct from feelings, commands, and useful recommendations. It then challenges the supplied substantiation with counterfactuals drawn from the background material.
How to use this audit

State the moral claim, choose every element you think an actual moral system must include, and explain how your own moral perspective substantiates each element.

  • Step 1: state the claim under audit as precisely as possible.
  • Step 2: select the elements you think are necessary and choose a substantiation route for each.
  • Step 3: read the boundary tests for collapse into emotion, obedience, or practical advice.
  • Step 4: review matched counterfactuals and cross-examination questions.
  • Step 5: export the report or AI prompt for a fuller stress test.
