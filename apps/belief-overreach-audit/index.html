Faith-gap audit

Belief Overreach Audit

Start with a fair die, where both symmetry and repeated experience support only a one-in-six chance that the next roll will be a six. Then watch what happens when confidence moves beyond that support and begins carrying plans, risks, and life commitments the evidence never supplied.

Not a verdict on God. This tool isolates a narrower question: if your confidence rises above the evidence you yourself think you have, what new failure pressure enters the picture?
How to use this audit

First, set your confidence about the next roll of a fair die being a six. The die fixes the support at 16.7%. The slider shows whether your belief stays inside that support or walks beyond it.

Next, run a short sequence of rolls. The tool compares what the evidence would warrant, what your confidence plans for, and what the world actually delivers.

Finally, transfer the same structure to faith-laden claims. Rate the support you think you really have, then rate how confident you are anyway. The gap is the part being carried by faith rather than perceived evidence.
